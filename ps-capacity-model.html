ⓘ Why Erlang C is the model behind voice & chat (click)
Erlang C is the telecom/contact-center industry-standard queueing model for inbound staffing. It treats calls as random (Poisson) arrivals into one shared queue and computes the probability a call must wait (Pw), then projected service level SL = 1 − Pw · e−(N−A)·(T/AHT), where offered load A = calls/hr × AHT(s)/3600, N = agents, T = answer-target seconds. Unlike a flat "volume ÷ AHT" estimate, it accounts for arrival randomness — so it does not undersize at the peak. (Engine reference-verified against the standard 200 calls/hr · 180s AHT · 80%/20s → 14-agent benchmark.)
