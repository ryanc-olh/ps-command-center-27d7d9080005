How these numbers are pulled
  • Email reasons (all clients): each week we take a random sample of 100 tickets per client from the previous complete week and read every ticket's actual message to classify why the patient contacted us. We don't use Zoho's built-in "category" field — it's blank on ~all email — so reasons are read from the real text.
  • What the % means: a reason's share of that client's 100-ticket sample (so columns total ~100%). It's a representative sample, not every ticket — directional for the mix, not an exact count.
  • Refresh: automatically every Monday ~6:30am MT. De-identified aggregates only — no patient info.
  • Phone vs email — different rigor: phone is a census of every answered call (Contact Lens tags all of them — an exact share, though only ~80–85% are categorized), while email is a ~100-ticket random sample per client (directional, subject to sampling error of a few points; email also mirrors from Zoho Desk with up to ~1-day lag). Read phone shares as exact; read email shares as approximate.
  • Phone reasons & sentiment: live from Amazon Connect Contact Lens on answered inbound calls — topic tags are multi-label (one call can raise several), so % is share of answered calls and can total >100%. Sentiment is the AWS call-end sentiment. Auto-refreshes daily. Phone and email mixes differ, so compare within a channel.
  • "Other" client: patient clients that don't have their own dashboard yet. ~55% of its email is non-patient noise (vendor/marketing/system — measured, n=250), so only ~45% is genuine patient contact; treat its patient reasons as a thin slice. (Built-client inboxes are far cleaner — see each client's measured real-patient rate on the Email view.)
